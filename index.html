About Us

Alabama Advanced Research Computing Consortium

The Alabama Advance Research Computing Consortium (AARCC) is an organization of skilled professionals committed to advancing research computing and data within Alabama's academic and research landscape. Key areas of focus for AARCC include fostering a community among research computing and data professionals, connecting with the wider research computing and data ecosystem, professional development, and identifying stakeholders and shared values in a time of rapid technological change.

The AARCC Charter outlines the following Vision and Mission:

Vision

AARCC propels research innovation by enhancing the capabilities of research computing and data (RCD) professionals in Alabama. This includes fostering their career progression and visibility, and their ability to offer effective services and resources to researchers. AARCC serves as a hub for RCD professionals and organizations, uniting them around common goals, facilitating knowledge exchange, and fostering ongoing advancements in research computing and data.

Mission

AARCC's communities, working groups, and collaborative partners drive the progression and sustainability of RCD at academic, government, and non-profit institutions in Alabama. AARCC unites professionals across various RCD roles and organizations, including system architects, software and data specialists, and those who collaborate directly with researchers. AARCC's working groups investigate and develop strategies, best practices, and products that empower researchers not just at the campus level, but also regionally, nationally, and internationally.

  • CI workforce development and professionalization. Focusing on advancing the capabilities and professional growth of individuals involved in computational infrastructure.
  • Connecting Alabama's research computing and data professional community. Promoting diverse roles such as researcher-facing, systems-facing, stakeholder-facing, and software/data-facing.
  • Identifying stakeholders and formulating value propositions. For the CI, research computing, and data communities in Alabama.
  • Understanding and shaping the future of research computing and data support. Advocacy and sustainability at campuses and beyond in Alabama.
  • Developing tools and resources. To define, support, and guide the profession of research computing and data within the academic sector.
