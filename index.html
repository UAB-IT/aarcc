Alabama Advanced Research Computing Consortium

The Alabama Advanced Research Computing Consortium (AARCC) is a strategic initiative addressing the gap in computational infrastructure (CI) and expertise in Alabama's higher education institutions. Despite national resources like XSEDE and the Open Science Grid, Alabama's research capability is hindered by limited local CI development, inadequate high-performance computing resources, and a shortage of research computing support. The AARCC aims to rectify this by unifying efforts across public, private, and HBCU doctoral institutions in Alabama. The goal is to secure sustained funding at local, state, and federal levels, enhancing CI and aligning with state political priorities and initiatives like the NSF CI CoE: Demo Pilot and Governor Kay Ivey’s Executive Order 720. This collaborative effort includes industry partners and regional and national CI organizations, leveraging this unique alignment to advance Alabama's research capacity, healthcare, and economic growth.

Intellectual Merit

The AARCC planning project will produce three deliverables:

  • An Alabama statewide research CI development plan
  • A statewide research network plan
  • A statewide research computing consortium organizational plan

Eight stakeholder engagement events will be held at the eight doctoral degree granting institutions in Alabama, two of which are designated HBCUs, and one event will be held with industry leaders in the state. These events will be an opportunity to inform researchers and campus leaders about the research CI available, but also for the project team to learn about research priorities and current barriers at those institutions. Representatives from the eight colleges/universities will form working groups to assure representation during plan development. A collaborative and coordinated approach is the best path to success given the institutional diversity, readiness, and priorities.

Broader Impacts

Alabama is at a unique moment in time where federal, state, and academic research priorities are poised to invest in a robust statewide CI for economic development and research goals. The eight institutions are representative of the entire AL higher education profile of 60 institutions and 15 HBCUs. The primary goal of AARCC is to increase Alabama researcher competitiveness by ensuring access to modern research CI and knowledge. Funding from the NSF for this project will provide credibility, authority, and an objective platform free of institution-specific motives for the stakeholders to move forward with an Alabama statewide initiative. AARCC will also achieve economies of scale for CI financial investments and knowledge dissemination for CI professional and research software engineering workforce development that individual institutions cannot. We seek Alabama research competitiveness in the NSF Big Ideas of Growing Convergence Research, Harnessing the Data Revolution, and Understanding the Rules of Life based on ALEPSCoR state priorities for investment.
